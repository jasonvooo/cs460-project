\n \n Britney Jean Spears\n \n (born December 2, 1981)\n \n \n [1]\n \n \n is an American singer, songwriter, dancer, and actress. Born in\n \n McComb, Mississippi\n \n and raised in\n \n Kentwood, Louisiana\n \n , she appeared in stage productions and television series, before signing with\n \n Jive Records\n \n in 1997. Spears\'s first two studio albums,\n \n \n ...Baby One More Time\n \n \n (1999) and\n \n \n Oops!... I Did It Again\n \n \n (2000), were global successes and made her the best-selling teenage artist of all-time. Referred to as the "\n \n Princess of Pop\n \n ", Spears was regarded as a\n \n pop icon\n \n \n \n [2]\n \n \n and credited with influencing the revival of\n \n teen pop\n \n during the late 1990s and early 2000s.\n

\n

\n Spears adopted more mature and provocative themes for her next two studio albums,\n \n \n Britney\n \n \n (2001) and\n \n \n In the Zone\n \n \n (2003), and made her feature film debut in a starring role in\n \n \n Crossroads\n \n \n (2002). Following a series of heavily publicized personal struggles and erratic public behavior, Spears\'s career was interrupted, before the release of her fifth studio album\n \n \n Blackout\n \n \n (2007), which is often critically referred to as her best work. Her erratic behavior and hospitalizations led Spears to be placed on a still ongoing\n \n conservatorship\n \n . She returned to the top of record charts with her sixth and seventh studio albums,\n \n \n Circus\n \n \n (2008) and\n \n \n Femme Fatale\n \n \n (2011), respectively. In 2012,\n \n \n Forbes\n \n \n reported that Spears was the highest paid female musician of the year, with earnings of $58million, having last topped the list in 2002.\n \n \n [3]\n \n \n During the promotion of her eighth and ninth studio albums,\n \n \n Britney Jean\n \n \n (2013) and\n \n \n Glory\n \n \n (2016), Spears embarked on a four-year\n \n concert residency\n \n ,\n \n Britney: Piece of Me\n \n , at\n \n Planet Hollywood Resort & Casino\n \n in Las Vegas, which became one of the highest-grossing residencies of all-time. In 2019, Spears announced an indefinite career hiatus due to her father\'s unstable health.\n

\n

\n Spears has scored six number one albums on the\n \n \n Billboard\n \n 200\n \n , making her the third best performing female artist on the chart.\n \n \n [4]\n \n \n Five of Spears\'s singles have reached number one on the US\n \n \n Billboard\n \n Hot 100\n \n : "\n \n ...Baby One More Time\n \n ", "\n \n Womanizer\n \n ", "\n \n 3\n \n ", "\n \n Hold It Against Me\n \n " and "\n \n S&M\n \n ". Other singles, "\n \n Oops!... I Did It Again\n \n " and "\n \n Toxic\n \n ", topped Australian and Canadian charts. With "3" in 2009 and "Hold It Against Me" in 2011, she became the second artist after\n \n Mariah Carey\n \n in the Hot 100\'s history to debut at number one with two or more songs. Spears has earned\n \n numerous awards\n \n and accolades, including a\n \n Grammy Award\n \n ; seven\n \n Guinness World Records\n \n ; six\n \n MTV Video Music Awards\n \n , including the\n \n Michael Jackson Video Vanguard Award\n \n ; seven\n \n \n Billboard\n \n Music Awards\n \n , including the Millennium Award;\n \n \n [5]\n \n \n the inaugural Radio Disney Icon Award; and a star on the\n \n Hollywood Walk of Fame\n \n .\n \n \n [6]\n \n \n \n Billboard\n \n ranked her as the eighth-biggest artist of the 2000s decade.\n \n \n [7]\n \n \n One of the world\'s\n \n best-selling music artists\n \n , Spears has sold over 150 million records worldwide\n \n \n [8]\n \n \n and more than 70 million records in United States, including 36.9 million digital singles and 33.6 million digital albums.\n \n \n [9]\n \n \n \n \n [10]\n \n \n In the United States, Spears is the fourth best-selling female album artist of the\n \n Nielsen SoundScan\n \n era\n \n \n [11]\n \n \n as well as the best-selling female album artist of the 2000s.\n \n \n [12]\n \n \n \n \n [13]\n \n \n \n \n [14]\n \n \n

\n \n \n \n 19811997: Early life and career beginnings\n \n

\n

\n Spears was born in 1981 in\n \n McComb, Mississippi\n \n , the second child of\n \n Lynne Irene Bridges\n \n and James Parnell Spears. Her maternal grandmother, Lillian Portell, was English (born in London), and one of Spears\'s maternal great-great-grandfathers was Maltese.\n \n \n [17]\n \n \n Her siblings are\n \n Bryan James\n \n and\n \n Jamie Lynn Spears\n \n .\n \n \n [18]\n \n \n Britney was born in the\n \n Bible Belt\n \n , where socially conservative\n \n evangelical Protestantism\n \n is a particularly strong religious influence.\n \n \n [19]\n \n \n She was baptized as a\n \n Southern Baptist\n \n , but as an adult has studied\n \n Kabbalist\n \n teachings.\n \n \n [20]\n \n \n She sang in a\n \n Baptist\n \n church choir as a child.\n \n \n [21]\n \n \n

\n

\n At age three, Britney started attending dance lessons in her hometown of\n \n Kentwood, Louisiana\n \n , and was selected to perform as a solo artist at the annual recital. Spears made her local stage debut at age five, singing "\n \n What Child Is This?\n \n " at her\n \n kindergarten\n \n graduation. During her childhood, she also had gymnastics and voice lessons, and won many state-level competitions and children\'s talent shows.\n \n \n [22]\n \n \n \n \n [23]\n \n \n \n \n [24]\n \n \n She said about her ambition as a child, "I was in my own world, [...] I found out what I\'m supposed to do at an early age".\n \n \n [23]\n \n \n

\n

\n At age eight, Spears and her mother Lynne traveled to\n \n Atlanta\n \n , Georgia to audition for the 1990s revival of\n \n \n The Mickey Mouse Club\n \n \n . Casting director Matt Casella rejected her as too young, but introduced her to Nancy Carson, a New York City talent agent. Carson was impressed with Spears\'s singing and suggested enrolling her at the\n \n Professional Performing Arts School\n \n ; shortly after, Lynne and her daughters moved to a sublet apartment in New York.\n

\n

\n Spears was hired for her first professional role as the understudy for the lead role of Tina Denmark in the Off-Broadway musical\n \n \n Ruthless!\n \n \n . She also appeared as a contestant on the popular television show\n \n \n Star Search\n \n \n and was cast in a number of commercials.\n \n \n [25]\n \n \n \n \n [26]\n \n \n In December 1992, she was cast in\n \n The Mickey Mouse Club\n \n alongside\n \n Christina Aguilera\n \n ,\n \n Justin Timberlake\n \n ,\n \n Ryan Gosling\n \n , and\n \n Keri Russell\n \n . After the show was canceled in 1996, she returned to Mississippi and enrolled at McComb\'s\n \n Parklane Academy\n \n . Although she made friends with most of her classmates, she compared the school to\n

\n In June 1997, Spears was in talks with manager\n \n Lou Pearlman\n \n to join the female pop group\n \n Innosense\n \n . Lynne asked family friend and entertainment lawyer\n \n Larry Rudolph\n \n for his opinion and submitted a tape of Spears singing over a\n \n Whitney Houston\n \n karaoke song along with some pictures. Rudolph decided that he wanted to pitch her to record labels, for which she needed a professional demo made. He sent Spears an unused song of\n \n Toni Braxton\n \n ; she rehearsed for a week and recorded her vocals in a studio with a\n \n sound engineer\n \n . Spears traveled to New York with the demo and met with executives from four labels, returning to Kentwood the same day. Three of the labels rejected her, saying that audiences wanted pop bands such as the\n \n Backstreet Boys\n \n and the\n \n Spice Girls\n \n , and "there wasn\'t going to be another\n \n Madonna\n \n , another\n \n Debbie Gibson\n \n , or another\n \n Tiffany\n \n ."\n \n \n [28]\n \n \n

\n

\n Two weeks later, executives from\n \n Jive Records\n \n returned calls to Rudolph.\n \n \n [28]\n \n \n Senior vice president of\n \n A&R\n \n Jeff Fenster said about Spears\'s audition that "it\'s very rare to hear someone that age who can deliver emotional content and commercial appeal...For any artist, the motivationthe \'eye of the tiger\'is extremely important. And Britney had that."\n \n \n [23]\n \n \n Spears sang Houston\'s "\n \n I Have Nothing\n \n " (1992) for the executives, and was subsequently signed to the label.\n \n \n [29]\n \n \n They appointed her to work with producer Eric Foster White for a month, who reportedly shaped her voice from "lower and less poppy" delivery to "distinctively, unmistakably Britney."\n \n \n [30]\n \n \n After hearing the recorded material, president\n \n Clive Calder\n \n ordered a full album. She felt secure with her label\'s appointment of producers, since "It made more sense to go pop, because I can dance to itit\'s more me."\n \n \n [23]\n \n \n She flew to\n \n Cheiron Studios\n \n in Stockholm, Sweden, where half of the album was recorded from March to April 1998, with producers\n \n Max Martin\n \n ,\n \n Denniz Pop\n \n and\n \n Rami Yacoub\n \n , among others.\n \n \n [23]\n \n \n

\n \n Rolling Stone\n \n magazine wrote about Spears: "One of the most controversial and successful female vocalists of the 21st century," she "spearheaded the rise of post-millennial teen pop... Spears early on cultivated a mixture of innocence and experience that generated lots of cash."\n \n \n [293]\n \n \n She is listed by the\n \n \n Guinness World Records\n \n \n as having the "Best-selling album by a teenage solo artist" for her debut album\n \n ...Baby One More Time\n \n which sold over thirteen million copies in the United States.\n \n \n [294]\n \n \n Melissa Ruggieri of the\n \n \n Richmond Times-Dispatch\n \n \n reported: "She\'s also marked for being the best-selling teenage artist. \n As of 2014\n \n \n [update]\n \n \n , she has sold over 100 million records worldwide, making her one of the\n \n best-selling music artists\n \n of all time.\n \n \n [10]\n \n \n She was also ranked as the fourth\n \n VH1\n \n \'s "50 Greatest Women of the Video Era" show list, ahead of most of her contemporaries and only behind veterans Madonna, Janet Jackson, and Whitney Houston.\n \n \n [296]\n \n \n Spears is also recognized as the best-selling female albums artist of the 2000s, as well as the fifth overall.\n \n \n [12]\n \n \n In December 2009,\n \n Billboard\n \n magazine ranked Spears the 8th Artist of the 2000s decade in the United States.\n \n \n [7]\n \n \n She is one of the few artists in history to have had a number one single and a number one studio album in the US during each of the three decades of her career.\n \n \n [297]\n \n \n

\n \n Philanthropy\n \n

\n

\n The singer founded The Britney Spears Foundation, a charitable entity set up to help children in need. The philosophy behind the Foundation was that music and entertainment has a healing quality that can benefit children.\n \n \n [357]\n \n \n The Foundation also supported the annual Britney Spears Camp for the Performing Arts, where campers had the opportunity to explore and develop their talents.\n \n \n [358]\n \n \n In April 2002, through the efforts of Spears and The Britney Spears Foundation, a grant of $1million was made to the Twin Towers Fund to support the children of uniformed service heroes affected by the\n \n terrorist attacks\n \n of September 11, 2001, including New York City Fire Department and its Emergency Medical Services Command, the New York City Police Department, the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey, the New York State Office of court Administration and other government offices.\n \n \n [359]\n \n \n However, it was reported in 2008 that the Foundation had a deficit of $200,000.\n \n \n [360]\n \n \n After the singer went through conservatorship, her father and lawyer Andrew Wallet zeroed out the effort, leading to its closure in 2011.\n \n \n [361]\n \n \n

\n

\n On October 30, 2001, Spears, alongside\n \n Bono\n \n and other popular recording artists under the name "Artists Against AIDS Worldwide", released a single titled "\n \n What\'s Going On\n \n ", with the intention to benefit AIDS programs in Africa and other impoverished regions.\n \n \n [362]\n \n \n In the wake of\n \n Hurricane Katrina\n \n in 2006, Spears donated $350,000 to\n \n Music Rising\n \n .\n \n \n [363]\n \n \n Later in 2011, the singer raised $200,000 during An Evening of Southern Style at a private residence in Beverly Hills to benefit the\n \n St. Bernard Project\n \n , with the help of several celebrities, including\n \n Hilary Duff\n \n ,\n \n Selena Gomez\n \n ,\n \n Kelly Osbourne\n \n ,\n \n Kellan Lutz\n \n and\n \n Kim Kardashian\n \n .\n \n \n [364]\n \n \n Spears has also helped several charities during her career, including Madonna\'s Kabbalah-based Spirituality for Kids,\n \n \n [365]\n \n \n cancer charity Gilda\'s Club Worldwide,\n \n \n [366]\n \n \n Promises Foundation, and\n \n United Way\n \n , with the latter two focused on giving families from various disadvantaged situations new hope and stable foundations for the future.\n \n \n [363]\n \n \n

\n

\n On October 24, 2015, Spears donated $120,000 to the Nevada Childhood Cancer Foundation.\n \n \n [367]\n \n \n In addition, $1 of each ticket sale for her Las Vegas residency,\n \n Britney: Piece of Me\n \n , was donated to the nonprofit organization.\n \n \n [368]\n \n \n Spears also fundraised for the charity through social media, in addition to selling limited edition merchandise, with all proceeds going to the NCCF.\n \n \n [369]\n \n \n On October 27, 2016, Spears partnered with\n \n Zappos\n \n and XCYCLE to host the\n \n Britney Spears Piece of Me Charity Ride\n \n in Boca Park, Las Vegas to raise additional money toward her goal of $1million for the NCCF, with $450,000 having already been raised from Spears\'s ticket sales and merchandise.\n \n \n [370]\n \n \n Participants were entered for a chance to win a spin class with Spears herself. The event ultimately went on to raise $553,130.\n \n \n [371]\n \n \n The fundraising ultimately led to the development of the NCCF Britney Spears Campus in Las Vegas, which saw its grand opening on November 4, 2017.\n \n \n [372]\n \n \n Spears also regularly participates in\n \n Spirit Day\n \n to combat bullying of\n \n LGBT\n \n Q youth and\n \n bullying\n \n .\n \n \n [373]\n \n \n
